News & Facts

Islam recognizes four major holy books, each revealed to a different prophet and serving as a guide for their respective communities. These scriptures are highly revered and provide valuable insights into the divine messages sent by Allah.

Watch Video

InterestingFacts

Kitab Taurat

Revealed to Prophet Musa (Moses):Approximately 3,300 years ago.
Foundational Text: Integral to Abrahamic religions.
Shared Stories: Includes tales also found in the Quran, such as Adam and Eve, Noah's Ark, and the Exodus from Egypt.

Kitab Injil

The Injil was revealed to Prophet Isa, known as Jesus in Christianity, who is highly revered in Islam as a prophet and messenger of Allah. The Injil emphasizes teachings of love, compassion, and forgiveness. It contains parables and lessons.

Kitab Zabur

The Kitab Zabur, also known as the Psalms, was revealed to Prophet Dawud. While the Zabur is specifically associated with Prophet Dawud. It holds significant importance in Jewish and Christian traditions as well, where it is known as the Book of Psalms in the Bible.

Al-Quran

The Quran is considered the final and most comprehensive revelation from Allah. It remains unchanged, word for word. The Quran contains numerous references to natural phenomena and scientific facts that have been confirmed by modern science.
