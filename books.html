Books of Kitab

In Islam, there are four significant holy books. The Injil, revealed to Prophet Isa, brings the teachings of compassion and love. The Zabur, given to Prophet Daud, contains profound hymns and praises. The Taurat, handed to Prophet Musa, offers foundational laws and guidance. Finally, the Al-Quran, revealed to Prophet Muhammad (saw), stands as the ultimate guide, encompassing ethics, law, and spirituality for all of humanity.

Get to know the 4 Kitab in Islam

Kitab Taurat

The Taurat, revealed to Prophet Musa (Moses), is one of the four holy books in Islam. It contains the laws and guidance given to the Israelites. Known for its emphasis on justice and also righteousness, It serves as a significant source of moral and legal teachings.

Kitab Injil

The Injil, revealed to Prophet Isa, is another of the four holy books in Islam. It contains the teachings and message of compassion, love, and guidance for humanity. The book of Injil emphasizes the importance of faith, charity, and also forgiveness.

Kitab Zabur

The Zabur, revealed to Prophet Daud, consists of sacred songs and hymns. This book explain about praise and devotion, emphasizing the importance of worship and gratitude to God. The Zabur also provides moral and spiritual guidance, encouraging believers to live righteously.

Al-Quran

The Al-Quran, revealed to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), is Islam's final and comprehensive holy book, offering guidance on faith, law, morality, and spirituality. It is revered for its linguistic beauty and serves as the unaltered word of God, central to Muslim faith and practice.
